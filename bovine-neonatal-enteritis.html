Chapter 8 Bovine Neonatal Enteritis



863 neonatal faecal samples were examined by AFBI in 2017. As in previous years Cryptosporidium was the most regularly identified pathogen, identified in 37% of cases, rotavirus was the second most regularly identified pathogen, identified in 30% of cases and coronavirus was identified in 11% of cases. Faecal samples sent to AFBI from calves less than two weeks of age are also checked for the presence of E.coli expressing the K99 fimbrial antigen. E.coli K99 has been associated with enterotoxigenic colibacillosis in neonates. In 2017 E.coli K99 was identified in 3% of faecal samples from calves less than 2 weeks of age.
